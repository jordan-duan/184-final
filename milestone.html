Updates

After two weeks, we have successfully begun the implementation of the two major parts of our project: shaders and spheres. More specifically, we implemented shaders using Fabric 1.20.2, a Minecraft mod loader. Fabric allowed us to easily add vertex, fragment, and GLSL files directly into Minecraft's native shaders folder. This resulted in us adding several of our milestone goals right away, including sky rendering with fog, stars, and clouds, shadow mapping with distortion, water rendering, physically-based rendering, reflection and ray marching, and deferred shading. We are planning to make a GUI or change keybinds to toggle different shaders with switch case functions (for example, 0=ambient, 1=diffusion, 2=specular, 3= blinn phong). We are also trying to tackle some edge cases of how light from torches react to sunlight and entity shadows, and shadow self collisions.

Relative to our original plan, we're roughly on schedule. We expected the shaders to be significantly more complicated, so the majority of our later milestones have already been completed. For the shading-based milestones that we haven't completed yet, we have a strong foundational understanding of how to implement shaders, meaning future development will hopefully go smoothly. In contrast, implementation of spheres was slightly more difficult than expected. While we were able to add them into Minecraft, we were left with a design choice: choosing between making the sphere a block or an entity. After we finish testing the pros and cons of each choice, we expect the rest of development to proceed smoothly.
